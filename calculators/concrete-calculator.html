How to Calculate Concrete

Concrete volume is measured in cubic yards in the US and cubic meters in the UK and most other countries. To find the volume of a rectangular slab, multiply length × width × thickness. A standard 4-inch residential driveway slab covering 10 ft × 12 ft requires approximately 1.48 cubic yards before adding a waste factor.

For round columns or tube forms, the calculator uses the formula for a cylinder: π × radius² × depth. Always add at least 10% for waste to account for spillage, uneven subgrade, and form irregularities.

When to Order Ready-Mix vs. Premix Bags

Ready-mix concrete (delivered by truck) is cost-effective for pours above 1 cubic yard. Below that threshold, premix bags from a home improvement store are often more practical. One 80 lb bag yields approximately 0.60 cubic feet of concrete.
