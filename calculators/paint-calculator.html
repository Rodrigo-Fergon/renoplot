How to Calculate How Much Paint You Need

The standard coverage for most interior latex paints is 350 square feet per gallon per coat. To estimate your project:

  1. Measure the length and width of your room.
  2. Multiply the perimeter (length + width × 2) by the ceiling height to get total wall area.
  3. Subtract 20 sq ft per door and 15 sq ft per window.
  4. Divide by 350 to get gallons per coat.
  5. Multiply by the number of coats.

Most rooms benefit from two coats, especially when painting over a dark color or bare drywall. Add 10% to your final number to account for touch-ups and waste.

Tips for Buying Paint

Paint is sold in quarts (1 qt = 0.25 gal) and gallons. If your estimate lands between sizes, round up. Leftover paint stores well for touch-ups if sealed properly.

For ceilings, use a dedicated ceiling paint — it is formulated to resist drips and usually requires only one coat.
