How to Use This Calculator

Select whether you need drywall for walls, ceiling, or both. Choose how you'd like to enter your measurements — by room perimeter, individual wall dimensions, or a total area you've already calculated.

Enter any openings (doors, windows, archways) to be deducted. Standard drywall sheets in the US measure 4×8 ft (32 sq ft), though 4×10 and 4×12 sheets are common for rooms with higher ceilings.

A 10% waste factor is recommended for most rooms. Increase it to 15–20% for rooms with complex angles, vaulted ceilings, or many cuts around openings.

Tips for Buying Drywall

Always round up to the nearest whole sheet — you can't buy fractions. Buy a few extra sheets to cover damage during transport or installation. ½-inch drywall is standard for interior walls; ⅝-inch is used for ceilings and fire-rated assemblies.
